Freshman Year


Honors Experiences this year were limited to Honors 100, and an Honors credit of CSE 390

Freshman Year, I was young, idealistic, and high off of getting into the UW two years early through the UW Robonson center for Young Scholars. I felt I could take on the world. At this point, I wanted to be a Computer Science major, and delved into my classes. At the start, I was doing fairly well for myself. But towrds spring and winter quarters, found myself performing subpar in my calculus classes. I began to grow a bit worried, and my high credit loads were taking their toll.

Autumn 2012

Honors 100

Honors 100 introduced me to the concept of Honors, and other honors students. Through interactions with other students, I found a welcoming community, and plenty of fun people.

CSE 390 H-Special Topics CSE

A lone honors credit, this course was a discussion format book club with Stuart Reges. We talked about the history of Computers, Turing Machines, and Encryption, as well as more contemporary topics such as Robotic companions for the elderly.. Generally, a platform for ideas and debates.

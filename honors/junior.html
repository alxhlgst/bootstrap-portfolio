Junior Year


Honors Experiences this year were an Honors section of ART 190, Intro to Drawing, three Quarters of Honors Physics, HONORS 394A: Comparative Ideology: Human Rights Movements, and JSIS 202, Cultural Interactions.
This year really exposed be to political and ideological thought, as well as the hard sciences, and I think really helped me understand the world better.

Maybe physics will boost by CS chances, I thought. Perhaps differential equasions? (Which I did not perform well in) Realizing my CS dreams were about finished after a few rejections, I began searching for a few last options. Maybe is I double with neurobiology, focusing on Brain-Computer Interfaces? Perhaps that interesting essay could do it. But alas, it was not to be. Around this point, I just had issues with motivation. Classes were not going as well as I envisioned, and I grew depressed for a time. Family troubles didn't help either, with pestering to get a job or move out. I went to therapy, which helped, but what helped most was when I accepted I was not getting into CS, and looked towards a n alternate path. HCDE was what I found, similar to CS, but with a bigger focus on the types of topics discussed in my Honors classes. A more well rounded alternitive. Two changes brought me out of my funk: Getting into HCDE, and getting a bussing job to get me out and about, nd bringing in some spending money.

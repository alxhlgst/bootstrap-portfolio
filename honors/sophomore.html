Spring 2014

HONORS 232 A: Of Pirates and Piracy- From Morgan's Bluff to Redmond?

This class was fascinating. Clark Speed was an eccentric professor, but in this class, I greatly expanded my concepts of Capitalism, Piracy, History, and Modern Society, as well as growing a much more detailed vocabulary. This was the first Honors class I took that really made me think differently, and I credit it to shaping many of the current thoughts I have today.
Three main papers were created for this class.

The first paper, Ignorance Is Power was an analysis of Treasure Island, and how Jim Hawkins' ignorance of pirate life was his greatest strength against Long John Silver

The second paper, Ship of Theseus explored the relationship between fetishization of ideas and copyright. I wrote it a few years ago at this point, so it's best to read it to understand it.

This third paper, Dimestone specifically talks about the role of capitalism, colonialism, and society. I am quite proud of this one. It builds the idea that Capitalism is a cycle, and much like limestone, there are layers of societies and commerece that rise and fall, as economic niches are filled, structure collapses, and the cycle begins anew.
